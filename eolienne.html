The Project

UrbanWind is an engineering project focused on designing a high-efficiency micro wind turbine for urban environments. Our goal was to convert wind energy into electricity through a compact, reliable, and cost-effective system. Spanning multiple development phases, this project involved a complete mechanical and structural design of the wind turbine‚Äôs key components, including the rotor, gearbox, shafts, and bearings. Through a mix of analytical calculations, CAD modeling, and finite element simulations, we engineered a custom transmission system that meets the strict performance and durability standards required for continuous daily operation over 10 years.

Milestone 1 ‚Äì System Design & Pre-Dimensioning

TIn the first phase of the project, we focused on establishing the foundation of the turbine‚Äôs design. This involved a comprehensive analysis of the energy conversion chain ‚Äî from wind to mechanical rotation ‚Äî and setting technical requirements based on environmental constraints and target energy output (4.9 kW). We dimensioned the rotor, calculated the blade length and rotor diameter using aerodynamic formulas, and designed the hollow shaft and belt transmission system. This stage culminated in validating the feasibility of the power transmission chain using real engineering data and catalogs.

Milestone 2 ‚Äì Modeling the Powertrain System

During the second phase, we transitioned from theoretical sizing to a concrete mechanical model using 3DX software. The core task was dimensioning the speed multiplier ‚Äî a crucial gearbox component connecting the rotor to the generator. We performed iterative calculations for gear selection using Hertz and bending stress criteria, considering material fatigue, torque transmission, and geometry optimization. Simultaneously, we selected and validated the ball bearings using SKF catalog data, ensuring long-term durability. The result was a fully modeled, feasible transmission system ready for virtual assembly.

Milestone 3 ‚Äì Structural Verification & CAD Completion

The final milestone focused on refining and verifying the mechanical components. We analytically and numerically verified shaft dimensions using ABAQUS finite element simulations, ensuring stress limits (Von Mises, Tresca) and maximum deflection criteria were met. We finalized the gearbox housing design with detailed CAD features such as split casings, sealing systems, and structural reinforcements. Additionally, we corrected the bearing selections to accommodate updated forces, and integrated all components into a clean, manufacturable assembly. This comprehensive validation ensures that UrbanWind is not only theoretically robust but practically buildable.
